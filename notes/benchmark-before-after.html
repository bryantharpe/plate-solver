What changed in each phase (code, not benchmark)

SP4 (the big win — 0.27× → 1.55× solve)

Replaced the eager combinations_4(n) -> Vec<[usize;4]> (which materialized every C(n,4) combo — up to ~618 MiB at n≈150 — before the solve loop could look at combo #1) with a lazy, allocation-free breadth-first iterator. 8/9 images match on combo #1–2, so solve time collapsed from single/tens-of-ms to sub-µs. combos_examined proved the win was laziness, not ordering.

FU-A (measurement + copy removal)

FUA.1: solve_from_image now self-reports real t_extract (was hardcoded 0.0). FUA.2: ExtractCentroids/SolveFromImage take the request's image buffer instead of .clone()-ing the full frame (~0.79 MB at 1024×768) — strictly less work, bit-for-bit identical output.

FU-B (profile + honest no-win)

FUB.1: profiled hale_bopp's NoMatch exhaustion (8855 combos, 2.15M DB lookups, 2778 slot hits): DB lookup ~51%, verify ~31%, candidate_keys build+sort ~15%. FUB.2: attempted 2 ps-solve allocation-reuse steps (candidate_keys Vec, A6 5-buffer hoist); both bit-for-bit green but neither statistically detectable (step-2: −1.8% mean, SNR<1 at n=12) — both reverted per the spec's "revert any step the measurement says didn't help." FUB.3: re-confirmed the baseline; no product code changed. The dominant cost (~66%) is in ps-db (lookup_pattern + nearby_stars) — a separate ps-db follow-up, not FUB.2's ps-solve scope.
